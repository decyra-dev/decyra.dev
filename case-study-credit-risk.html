AI-Driven Credit Risk Decision Platform

Transforming manual underwriting into AI-assisted decision systems

Industry: Banking & Financial Services

This engagement focused on modernizing credit risk evaluation by augmenting traditional underwriting with AI-driven insights.

The Problem

Credit risk analysts were manually reviewing financial statements, customer data and supporting documents. This resulted in slow decision cycles, inconsistent evaluations and limited scalability.

Existing systems relied heavily on static rules and reports, lacking the ability to combine structured financial data with unstructured documents for contextual decision-making.

The Approach

Designed an AI-assisted decision platform combining enterprise data with LLM-driven analysis to support faster and more consistent credit risk evaluation.

  • Integrated financial data, customer profiles and document sources
  • Implemented RAG pipelines for contextual document understanding
  • Enabled LLM-based summarization of financial risk signals
  • Designed human-in-the-loop workflows for validation and governance

Architecture Highlights

  • Snowflake-based governed data platform for unified data access
  • Secure ingestion of financial and document data
  • Vector-based retrieval for contextual analysis
  • LLM integration for risk summarization and insights
  • Workflow orchestration for underwriting processes

Architecture Considerations

  • Balancing explainability vs model-driven insights
  • Ensuring regulatory compliance and auditability
  • Maintaining data security and controlled access
  • Reducing hallucination risk in financial decision contexts

Outcome

  • 60–70% reduction in credit decision turnaround time
  • Improved consistency in risk assessment across analysts
  • Scalable underwriting operations with AI assistance
  • Enhanced ability to process complex financial data and documents

Key Insight

The biggest shift was not automating underwriting, but augmenting decision-making with AI while preserving human oversight and regulatory control.
